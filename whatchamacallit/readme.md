# 🟡 Whatchamacallit

**DIY Tools for Design Research with Very Rapid Prototyping**

## **🧭 Overview**

Whatchamacallit is a design research approach based on rapid, intuitive making. Instead of relying on observation, interviews, or predefined methods, it uses physical objects as tools to explore and interpret the world through direct experience.

The process emphasizes action over planning, allowing ideas to emerge during fabrication and interaction.

- Research happens through making, not only through analysis
- Objects act as mediators between body and environment
- The process values speed, intuition, and exploration over precision

---

## **💡 Concept**

The project questions the idea that design research must be structured, rational, and language-driven. It proposes a more open approach, where ambiguity and physical interaction play a central role.

The “whatchamacallit” is intentionally undefined, allowing each object to evolve without a fixed goal or function.

- Making is treated as a form of thinking
- The body becomes an active research instrument
- Ambiguity is used as a driver for discovery, not as a limitation

This shifts the focus from solving problems to generating new perspectives.

---

## **⚙️ How It Works**

The process is deliberately simple and avoids predefined steps. It encourages immediate action, letting the object develop through continuous interaction between idea, material, and context.

Participants start from a vague intuition and move directly into making, using the object to explore a situation physically.

- Begin with an abstract idea, question, or feeling
- Build immediately, without sketches or planning
- Use the object to interact with a real environment
- Observe how perception changes through use

The result is not just a prototype, but a tool for interpretation.

---

## **🛠️ Materials & Fabrication**

The fabrication process is intentionally rough and accessible. Materials are chosen for their availability and ease of manipulation, not for their performance.

This removes technical barriers and keeps the focus on exploration.

- Cardboard, paper, and packaging materials
- Chopsticks, sticks, and simple structural elements
- Tape, glue, and quick assembly methods
- Reused or discarded materials whenever possible

Precision is not relevant, speed and adaptability are more important.

---

## **🌍 Application Scenarios**

This approach can be applied in a wide range of exploratory contexts, especially where traditional research methods are limited.

It is particularly useful when dealing with perception, scale, or non-human perspectives.

- Exploring natural or ecological environments
- Simulating alternative viewpoints, such as animals or insects
- Investigating spatial perception and body awareness
- Developing speculative or experiential design concepts

The method is flexible and adapts to different disciplines and questions.

---

## **🎓 Teaching Value**

Whatchamacallit is highly effective in educational settings, especially in design and creative disciplines. It creates a low-pressure environment where experimentation becomes natural.

Students engage directly with materials and ideas, bypassing the need for fully defined concepts.

- Encourages rapid prototyping without fear of failure
- Helps externalize abstract ideas into tangible forms
- Promotes learning through doing rather than explaining
- Supports curiosity, play, and exploration

This makes it particularly suitable for early-stage design processes.

---

## **🧠 Key Insights**

The method highlights how knowledge can emerge from action and interaction, rather than from predefined frameworks.

- Making can replace verbal analysis in early research stages
- Physical interaction reveals hidden variables and relationships
- Ambiguity can generate richer and more unexpected outcomes
- Tools can function as cognitive devices, not only as functional objects

---

## **🔄 Possible Extensions**

The approach can be expanded by integrating additional layers of technology or documentation, while preserving its experimental nature.

- Integrate sensors and microcontrollers, such as Arduino
- Record interactions through video or motion tracking
- Combine with AI tools for interpretation or pattern analysis
- Use in interdisciplinary workshops or research contexts

These extensions can increase depth without removing spontaneity.
